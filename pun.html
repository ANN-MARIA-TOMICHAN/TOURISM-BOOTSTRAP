Explore Punjab

The Heart of India - Rich Culture, Traditions, and Vibrant History

Punjab, known as the "Land of Five Rivers," is a state located in the northern part of India. Famous for its agriculture, vibrant culture, and historical significance, Punjab offers an unforgettable experience to visitors. The Golden Temple, the sacred Harmandir Sahib, in Amritsar, is one of the holiest sites for Sikhs, and the state is also known for its rich tradition of dance, music, and the famous Punjabi cuisine. Punjab is a blend of modernity and tradition, offering everything from bustling cities to peaceful villages, along with its iconic festivals and warm hospitality.

Top Attractions in Punjab

Golden Temple

Located in Amritsar, the Golden Temple is one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Sikhs and a symbol of peace and spirituality.

Wagah Border

Wagah Border is famous for the daily flag-lowering ceremony, where the Indian and Pakistani forces engage in a show of strength and patriotism.

Jallianwala Bagh

This historical garden in Amritsar is a site of national importance, where hundreds of innocent people were killed during the British colonial era.
